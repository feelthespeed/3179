The Tennis Big 3

Roger Federer, Rafael Nadal, and Novak Djokovic have dominated
the world tennis landscape for over 20 years. But how good are they really?

Champions

Djokovic, Nadal and Federer are the three greatest
all-time record holders for grand slams.
Pete Sampras is closest to their record, but has six less titles.
Australian legend Rod Laver has 12 titles to his name.

A Global Sport

Tennis is played all around the world on a variety of surfaces including grass, clay and hard court. The tour includes over 50 events across the calendar year, meaning the Big 3 need to adapt to different environments around the world. The grand slams are played on different surfaces, with the Australian Open and US Open on hard court, with clay for the French Open and grass for Wimbledon.

Australia holds a number of tournaments in Melbourne, Adelaide, Hobart and Brisbane. Due to COVID-19, events in Brisbane were paused for 2022. Melbourne is the home of the Australian Open, the first grand slam of the year, which takes place during the last two weeks of January at the height of the Australian summer.

Totally Dominant

The Big 3 have claimed top spot in the world of tennis since 2003. Their presence at the top of the tennis world has been a constant motivator for rivals around the world. Federer was the first to climb to the top, with Nadal close behind. In more recent times, however, Djokovic has extended his lead over the rest.

Slam Specialists

Federer, Nadal and Djokovic have had their fair share of wins, but each have a specific tournament where they excelled. For Djokovic, this is the Australian Open. Federer, Wimbledon. For Nadal, the French Open. His performances there alone would be enough for him to secure third on the all-time total grand slam leaderboard.

Claiming The Majority

From the year 2000 onwards, Djokovic, Federer and Nadal have claimed the majority of tennis grand slam titles awarded to the present day, claiming 66/91 (72.95%) titles. Andy Murray and Stan Wawrinka are the most succesful of the rest in this period, with three titles each.

Surface Strengths

Consistent with their performances in grand slams, each of the Big 3 had particular strengths on different surfaces. Nadal was the clay court king. Federer was excellent on grass and Djokovic performed well on hard surfaces. Across all surfaces combined, Djokovic retains the best all-time career index (wins/matches) record.


A Constant Presence

The Big 3 have claimed at least one grand slam in every calendar year since 2003. Federer went on the greatest winning streak from 2003 to 2007, winning at least two grand slam titles for four consecutive years

2017 was a particularly interesting year as Djokovic was sidelined with an elbow injury and Federer and Nadal came together for a classic Australian Open final with Nadal not having won a grand slam title since 2014 at that point and Federer in 2012.

Time has taken its toll on Federer and Nadal, with Federer retiring from the professional tour in 2022 and Nadal currently taking a break in an attempt to heal a hip injury. Success continues to come Djokovic's way, with three titles won in 2023.

One Still Stands

The Big 3 were an unmatched force at the top. Two generations were unable to crack their dominance. Their run was unprecedented and while there may be many greats in the future that exceed their grand slam records, it is unlikely they will do so all at the same time. While Federer and Nadal have seemingly ended their reign at the top, Djokovic seems as powerful as ever.

Will anyone be able to topple him? And how far will he go?
