The Tennis Big-3

The tennis Big-3 and their performances around the world

Djokovic, Nadal and Federer are the three greatest all-time record holders for grand slams.
Pete Sampras is closest to their record, but has six less titles

A Global Sport

Tennis is played all around the world on a variety of surfaces including Grass, Clay and Hard Court. Carpet courts were also used in the ATP Tour until 2009. Of the grand slams (Australian Open, French Open, Wimbledon and US Open), the Australian Open and US Open use Hard court, with Clay used for the French Open and Grass for Wimbledon.

Surface Champion

Despite their dominance across tournaments and grand slams, each of them had particular strengths on different surfaces. Nadal, for example was the undisputed clay court king. Across all surfaces, Djokovic retains the best all-time win-loss record.

Totally Dominant

Since 2003, three sporting greats have dominated the world of tennis: Roger Federer, Rafael Nadal and Novak Djokovic. Their presence at the top of the tennis world has been a constant motivator for rivals around the world. In more recent times, however, one of them has extended their lead over the rest.

A Constant Presence

The Big 3 have not claimed at least one grand slam in a calendar year since 2003. Federer went on the greatest winning streak from 2003 to 2007, winning at least two grand slam titles for four consectuive years

Claiming The Majority

From the year 2000 onwards, Djokovic, Federer and Nadal have claimed the majority of tennis grand slam titles awarded to the present day
